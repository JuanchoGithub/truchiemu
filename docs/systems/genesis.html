Hardware Overview

The Sega Genesis, known as Mega Drive outside North America, launched in 1988 (Japan) with a Motorola 68000 CPU running at 7.67 MHz. This 16-bit processor, paired with a Zilog Z80 coprocessor at 3.58 MHz for audio, created a powerful architecture that competed directly with the SNES.

The graphics system featured Tile and Sprite engines capable of displaying 64 sprites with max dimensions of 32x32 pixels, and up to 80 sprites total. Resolution modes included 320x224 pixels (H40 mode) and 256x224 pixels (H32 mode), with a master palette of 512 colors including highlight and shadow effects.

Audio came courtesy of the Yamaha YM2612, a 6-channel FM synthesizer plus the Texas Instruments SN76489 PSG providing 3 square wave channels and 1 noise channel. The Z80 coprocessor accessed 8KB of dedicated audio RAM, enabling sophisticated compositions from artists like Yuzo Koshiro.

Memory included 64KB main RAM, 64KB Video RAM, and 8KB Audio RAM. The cartridge system supported various enhancement chips including the Sega Virtua Processor (SVP) for enhanced 3D gameplay in titles like Virtua Racing.

Notable features included hardware scrolling across two planes, tilemaps for backgrounds and foregrounds, extensive sprite capabilities, and backward compatibility with Master System through the Power Base Converter.

Known Issues and Workarounds

Sonic 3 Lock-On Technology:

Issue: Lock-On cartridges with Sonic & Knuckles may not work properly.

Workaround: Use specific patches or load games separately. Proper ROM header merging required.

Sega CD FMV Sync:

Issue: FMV cutscenes may have audio/video sync issues.

Workaround: Enable "CD Load Speed" adjustment or use bios delay settings.

32X Compatibility:

Issue: 32X games generally have poor compatibility across all cores.

Workaround: Use picodrive for best 32X support, but expect glitches and crashes.
