Sega Genesis / Mega Drive

The Sega Genesis, known as Mega Drive outside North America, launched in 1988 (Japan) with a Motorola 68000 CPU running at 7.67 MHz. This 16-bit processor, paired with a Zilog Z80 coprocessor at 3.58 MHz for audio, created a powerful architecture that competed directly with the SNES.

Hardware Overview

The graphics system featured Tile and Sprite engines capable of displaying 64 sprites with max dimensions of 32x32 pixels. Resolution modes included 320x224 pixels (H40 mode) and 256x224 pixels (H32 mode), with a master palette of 512 colors including highlight and shadow effects.

Audio came courtesy of the Yamaha YM2612, a 6-channel FM synthesizer plus the Texas Instruments SN76489 PSG providing 3 square wave channels and 1 noise channel.

Memory included 64KB main RAM, 64KB Video RAM, and 8KB Audio RAM. The cartridge system supported various enhancement chips including the Sega Virtua Processor (SVP) for enhanced 3D gameplay.

Known Issues

Sonic 3 Lock-On Technology

Lock-On cartridges with Sonic & Knuckles may not work properly. Use specific patches or load games separately.

Sega CD FMV Sync

FMV cutscenes may have audio/video sync issues. Enable "CD Load Speed" adjustment in core options.

32X Compatibility

32X games have variable compatibility. Use picodrive for best 32X support.
